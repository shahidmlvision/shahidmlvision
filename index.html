🎓 About Me

I am a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Action Postdoctoral Fellow at University College Dublin, developing next-generation vision-based frameworks for objective movement analysis in sports and patient-centred motor assessment for neuromuscular diseases.

My research focuses on markerless motion capture using single and multi-camera mobile phone systems, enabling accurate, scalable, and accessible biomechanical measurements outside traditional laboratory environments.

I am particularly interested in developing effective acquisition protocols and computational methods for human movement assessment in real-world settings, including:

  • 🏃 Sports Performance Analysis
  • 🏥 Neuromusculoskeletal Injury Assessment
  • 🔄 Rehabilitation
  • 🚶 Clinical Gait Evaluation

🔬 Research Approach

By combining Computer Vision, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Biomechanics, I create patient-centred and athlete-centred technologies that provide objective, quantitative insights into:

  • Movement Quality Assessment
  • Motor Impairments Detection
  • Functional Performance Metrics

🎯 Mission

My goal is to bridge the gap between laboratory-grade motion analysis and practical field deployment, making advanced movement assessment accessible through everyday mobile devices. This enables clinicians, researchers, coaches, and patients to make data-driven decisions for better health and athletic outcomes.
