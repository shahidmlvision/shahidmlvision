## 👋 About Me

I am a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Action Postdoctoral Fellow at **University College Dublin**, where I am developing next-generation vision-based frameworks for objective movement analysis in sports and patient-centred motor assessment for neuromuscular diseases.

My research focuses on **markerless motion capture** using single and multi-camera mobile phone systems, enabling accurate, scalable, and accessible biomechanical measurements outside traditional laboratory environments. I am particularly interested in developing effective acquisition protocols and computational methods for human movement assessment in real-world settings, including:

- 🏃 Sports Performance Analysis
- 🏥 Neuromusculoskeletal Injury Assessment
- 🔄 Rehabilitation
- 🚶 Clinical Gait Evaluation

---

## 🔬 Research Approach

By combining **Computer Vision**, **Machine Learning**, **Artificial Intelligence**, and **Biomechanics**, I work on creating **patient-centred and athlete-centred technologies** that provide objective, quantitative insights into:

- Movement Quality
- Motor Impairments
- Functional Performance

My goal is to **bridge the gap between laboratory-grade motion analysis and practical field deployment**, making advanced movement assessment accessible through everyday mobile devices.
